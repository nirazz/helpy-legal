Section 1

What is an Assumption Map?

Every product is built on beliefs. Some are explicit. Most are invisible. The dangerous ones are assumptions that are both high-risk (the product fails if they're wrong) and untested (we don't actually know). This map surfaces them before they sink the ship.

💡
Why this matters at Helpy's stage
With 16 users at launch, nearly everything is still an assumption. The goal isn't to validate everything - it's to run the cheapest, fastest test on the assumption most likely to kill the product. Prioritize relentlessly.
Danger Zone
High risk + Untested
Act immediately. Run the cheapest test that could invalidate this. Every week you delay is risk.
Validated
High risk + Tested (proven true)
These were scary and you proved them. Use as conviction for investors and your team. Keep evidence fresh.
Watch
Low risk + Untested
Test opportunistically. These matter but won't kill you if they're wrong. Add to the queue, not the sprint.
Safe
Low risk + Tested
These are your foundation. Document them so new team members start with context, not guesswork.

Section 3

Top 3 Assumptions to Test Next

These are pulled from the Danger Zone. Ranked by how quickly they could kill the product if wrong. Run the cheapest test that would give you a clear signal.

1
P0 - Test This Week
"Neighbors are willing to help strangers for free, regularly"
Why It Matters
If helpers only engage once out of novelty, DAU collapses. The whole supply side depends on intrinsic motivation - not gamification or money.
How to Test
Look at repeat helper behavior in DB. Did any user offer help more than 3 times? Interview 3 helpers: "What keeps you coming back?"
Success Criteria
At least 30% of helpers who completed one match create a second offer within 30 days. User interviews confirm intrinsic motivation, not just curiosity.
2
P1 - Test This Month
"Tel Aviv has enough density for the feed to feel active at 200 users"
Why It Matters
An empty feed kills new users immediately. The cold-start problem is existential for any marketplace at this stage.
How to Test
Simulate: how many requests are visible to a user with 10km radius in central Tel Aviv right now? Track "cards seen per session" in PostHog. Ask new users: did the feed feel active?
Success Criteria
New users see at least 5 cards in their first session. Less than 20% of sessions end at an empty state within the first week of signup.
3
P2 - Test Next Quarter
"Hebrew-speaking users prefer Helpy over WhatsApp neighborhood groups"
Why It Matters
WhatsApp groups are the incumbent. If users default to WhatsApp for anything Helpy does, growth stalls. We need to understand the specific jobs Helpy wins vs. loses.
How to Test
User interview question: "Before posting on Helpy, did you try WhatsApp first? Why or why not?" Track source attribution if possible.
Success Criteria
At least 60% of requesters say they would NOT have gotten this help via WhatsApp. Helpy fills a gap, not a duplicate channel.
