User Interview Script

Discovery questions for Helpy's first real users. Understand the problem - don't pitch the solution.

Section 1

Interview Setup

Get the logistics right before the first question. Good setup equals better signal.

⌛
Duration
45 - 60 minutes
📹
Format
Video or in-person (record with permission)
👥
Team
2 interviewers: 1 asks, 1 takes notes
🎯
Goal
Understand the real problem, not validate assumptions
⚠️
Golden Rule
"You are not selling. You are learning. Never pitch during an interview."
🔎
Who to recruit: Active users who posted a request OR offered help at least once. Cold users give you politeness, not insight.
Section 2

Warm-Up Questions ⌛ 5 min

Build rapport and get context before diving into the real problem space. Listen for neighborhood tenure, digital habits, and social connectedness.

1
Tell me about yourself - where do you live, and what do you do day to day?
2
How long have you lived in your current neighborhood or building?
3
How connected do you feel to your neighbors? (1 = total strangers, 10 = close community)
1 - Strangers
10 - Community
4
What apps do you use daily to communicate with people nearby - building chats, neighborhood groups, anything like that?
5
When was the last time you needed help with something small - something you couldn't or didn't want to do alone?
Section 3

Core Discovery Questions ⌛ 25 min

These are the high-signal questions. Follow threads. If someone says something interesting, go deeper - "tell me more about that" beats moving to the next question.

The Problem
1
Walk me through the last time you needed help from someone nearby. What happened, start to finish?
2
What did you try first? How did that go?
3
How did that make you feel when you had to ask - or when you couldn't bring yourself to ask?
4
What stopped you from asking your neighbor directly?
5
How often does this kind of situation come up - needing small help and not knowing who to ask?
The Behavior
1
Before Helpy, what was your go-to method for getting or giving local help?
2
Tell me about a time you helped a neighbor - someone you might not have known well. How did that feel?
3
What made you trust - or not trust - someone enough to help them or accept their help?
4
How do you currently know who in your building or street you could actually ask for something?
Helpy Specific
1
Walk me through the first time you used Helpy. What happened?
2
What made you come back after the first time?
3
What almost stopped you from using it - what friction did you feel?
4
When did Helpy feel most valuable? When did it feel least valuable?
5
If Helpy disappeared tomorrow, what would you do instead?
Section 4

The PMF Question

The Sean Ellis question. Ask it directly, verbally, after Section 3. Note the spontaneous reaction before they consciously answer.

The Sean Ellis Test - tap to mark an answer
"How would you feel if you could no longer use Helpy?"
😔 Very disappointed
😕 Somewhat disappointed
😐 Not disappointed
❎ N/A - I've already stopped using it
📊
PMF signal: more than 40% "Very disappointed" across your cohort is the benchmark from Sean Ellis's research. Below 40% means optimize before scaling. Above 40% means you've found product-market fit - now grow. Segment by user type: PMF may exist within a specific cohort even if the overall number is low.
Section 5

Wrap-Up ⌛ 10 min

End on open-ended reflections. Often the most valuable quotes come in the last 5 minutes when the interviewee relaxes and says what they really think.

1
Is there anyone else you think we should talk to?
2
What's the one thing we should absolutely fix?
3
What's the one thing we should never change?
4
Is there anything I didn't ask that you think is important for us to know?
