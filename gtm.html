GTM Philosophy

Helpy is a hyper-local product. Growth in a neighborhood app doesn't come from Facebook ads - it comes from density. One fully activated neighborhood (50+ users within 1km) is worth more than 500 scattered installs across the city. The strategy is neighborhood-by-neighborhood activation, not city-wide spray. You win when a critical mass of people in a small area all know the app exists - and the only way to get there is to show up in the places where those people already talk: WhatsApp groups, Facebook communities, and trusted word of mouth. Every growth dollar and every hour of founder time should be concentrated until one neighborhood reaches escape velocity.

Acquisition Channels

Tier 1 - High ROI (Free)
🗣️
Word of Mouth / Invite Feature CAC = $0
Every user gets a bilingual share card via the native share sheet. The invite card reads "I'm on Helpy - your neighbors can help with almost anything. Join me:" + App Store link. Best acquisition channel bar none - a trusted referral converts 3-5x better than any ad. The streak and helper tier systems create natural reasons to invite ("help me reach my weekly goal").
💬
WhatsApp Group Seeding Free
Post in 5-10 neighborhood WhatsApp groups per target neighborhood. Message must be personal, not spammy. One post per group, never spam. Get a local contact to post it - "neighbor introducing app" converts far better than "founder pitching app."
"שלום לכולם! מצאתי אפליקציה שנראית מעולה לשכונה שלנו - Helpy. בסיסית היא ענה על הבקשות האלה שתמיד מופיעות פה בקבוצה. אפשר לבקש עזרה מהשכנים ולהציע עזרה בקלות, בלי להציף את הקבוצה. מנסה אתכם? 👉 [App Store link]"
👥
Neighborhood Facebook Groups Free
Same core message as WhatsApp but adapted for Facebook's longer-form format. Include 2-3 app screenshots directly in the post. Tag the post with relevant community keywords. Visual posts with real screenshots consistently outperform text-only posts in community groups - lead with the browse screen so people can see real requests.
Tier 2 - Medium ROI (Low cost)
🔍
App Store Optimization (ASO) Low effort
Hebrew keywords: עזרה שכנים, עזרה שכנותית, עזרה בשכונה, שכנות, קהילה מקומית. English keywords: neighbors help, community help, local help app, neighborhood assistance, mutual aid. Subtitle should rotate seasonally. Respond to all reviews within 24 hours - review velocity signals App Store algorithm.
🏅
Ambassador Program - Neighborhood Captains No cost
Recruit 1 "neighborhood captain" per target neighborhood. They don't get paid - they get recognition, early access, and the identity of "the person who brought Helpy to Florentin." Ask them to seed 10 friends personally and post in WhatsApp groups. Identity-based motivation ("I'm a community leader") is more durable than cash. Look for: active community Facebook group members, HOA participants, or local business owners.
📰
Local Community Blogs and Forums Low cost
Reach out to local Tel Aviv community writers and bloggers. Offer an exclusive "first look" story framed as "local founder builds neighborhood app in Tel Aviv." Target: Geektime (Israeli tech blog), CTech, local Tel Aviv neighborhood blogs. A single feature article can drive 50-100 targeted installs from the right audience.
Tier 3 - Later (Paid, after PMF)
📱
Instagram / TikTok Organic Content Defer
User stories in "before/after" format: "I needed help moving a couch at 7pm - my neighbor showed up in 20 minutes." Build social proof before running ads. Don't invest here until you have 5+ genuine user stories to share.
🍎
Apple Search Ads After ASO
Only after ASO keyword targeting is dialed in. Start with exact-match on proven organic keywords. Cap at $5/day per keyword while learning CAC. Do not run broad match without budget controls.
🎯
Paid Social Defer
Defer until CAC/LTV ratio is understood from organic channels. A neighborhood app with no density in the target area will show negative ROAS no matter how good the creative. Earn your density first.

Key Risks

🚫 WhatsApp Groups Fight Back
Community admins may resist or remove posts promoting outside apps. Some groups have strict no-promotion rules. A ban from a key neighborhood group could block a major acquisition channel.
Position as complement, not replacement: "For the requests that flood your group, now they have a home." Use a neighbor as your proxy poster - not you, the founder.
📵 Phone Verification Drop-off
Users abandon at OTP step - especially on Android or older iOS. High drop-off between install and verified user tanks every downstream metric. Current flow: sign-in then verify on first action.
A/B test verification timing: gate at sign-in vs gate at first swipe right. Measure which version has higher D3 retention. Also test SMS copy - Hebrew vs English OTP message.
🏜️ Empty Neighborhood (No Critical Mass)
If users open the browse feed and see 0-2 nearby requests, they leave and don't return. A cold-start neighborhood with no density kills retention before the product gets a fair chance.
Focus 100% on Florentin first. Don't expand to Dizengoff until Florentin has 30+ active users. Seed with realistic sample requests if needed to show early adopters the intended experience.
