The Method
🧠

What is a Pre-Mortem?

A pre-mortem is the opposite of a post-mortem. Instead of analyzing failure after it happens, you assume the project has already failed and work backwards to figure out why. This forces you to confront uncomfortable risks before you're emotionally invested - when it's still cheap to change course. It's one of the most underused tools in product development.

"The time to repair the roof is when the sun is shining." - John F. Kennedy
💀

The Exercise

It is October 2027. Helpy has been shut down. The app has been removed from the App Store. The Supabase project is paused. The domain has lapsed. 18 months of work came to nothing. What went wrong? Walk through each scenario below and ask: could this happen to us?

The Six Obituaries
📭
HIGH LIKELIHOOD

Scenario 1 - The Empty Feed

The cold start problem killed us
Helpy never reached critical mass in any neighborhood. Users opened the app, saw 2-3 stale requests from 8km away in a category they couldn't help with, and never came back. The feed felt empty. Without enough supply, demand dried up. A great product killed by a distribution problem, not a product problem. The app had a 4.5-star rating from the 12 users who actually used it - but there were only 12 of them.
Prevention Actions
Hyper-focus on ONE Tel Aviv neighborhood first - flood it with density before expanding
Manually seed content in early weeks (Nir posts real requests, recruits neighbors directly)
Ambassador program: 3 paid neighborhood reps post weekly, activate their buildings
Track "feed density" metric (avg cards seen per session) before any expansion
🔐
MEDIUM LIKELIHOOD

Scenario 2 - The Trust Incident

One bad actor destroyed everything
A helper used Helpy to gain entry to an elderly woman's apartment under the pretense of helping with a technical issue, then stole valuables. The story was covered by Ynet and Walla. Downloads dropped 90% in 48 hours. Despite having a report system, content moderation, and terms of service, the PR damage was permanent. The Israeli market is small - word travels fast. No one wanted to be associated with the app that enabled elder fraud.
Prevention Actions
Add ID verification option for "sensitive" categories (elderly-care, home access)
Faster manual moderation - respond to reports within 4 hours, not 24
Clear safety guidelines shown before any in-person meetup is arranged
Liability framework in ToS - regularly reviewed by a lawyer at 500+ users
💬
MEDIUM LIKELIHOOD

Scenario 3 - WhatsApp Copies It

Distribution beat product
WhatsApp launched "Neighborhood Help" in May 2026 - a lightweight feature inside existing neighborhood groups that let you post requests and offer help with a thumbs-up. It wasn't as good as Helpy: no matching algorithm, no ratings, no privacy controls. But it was already on every single phone in Israel. Helpy's DAU dropped 60% in 30 days. No one downloads a new app when the feature is "good enough" in an app they already have open 40 times a day.
Prevention Actions
Build deep trust features WhatsApp cannot copy: verified identity, ratings history, urgency routing
Differentiate on safety and accountability - your neighbor's verified track record is the moat
Own the "trusted neighborhood helper" positioning, not just the "request posting" use case
At PMF signal, raise a small round to accelerate before platforms can react
🔋
MEDIUM LIKELIHOOD

Scenario 4 - Founder Burned Out

Solo founder unsustainability
Nir was doing everything - product, development (with Claude), marketing, support, legal, hosting, App Store submissions, moderation. 14 months in, 150 users, no revenue. A full-time job on top of this. The grind was 2-3 hours every night and every weekend. Without a co-founder to share the cognitive and emotional load, it became unsustainable. One bad week - a rejection, a bug, a slow month - was enough to tip the scale. The app went unmaintained. Users stopped coming.
Prevention Actions
Actively look for a co-founder who owns either tech or growth/community
Define a personal sustainability threshold upfront: if below X users by Y date, evaluate options honestly
Explore pre-seed funding at 500 MAU - even $30K buys 6 months of runway and validation
Keep the maintenance burden low: use managed infra (Supabase, EAS) over self-hosted anything
🍎
LOW-MEDIUM LIKELIHOOD

Scenario 5 - App Store Policy Change

Platform risk materialized
Apple updated its UGC guidelines in Q3 2026, requiring all apps that facilitate in-person meetings between strangers to implement AI-powered content moderation reviewed by a human trust and safety team within 24 hours, plus a $50,000/year safety compliance certification. It was aimed at large platforms but written broadly enough to catch Helpy. The app was removed from the App Store pending compliance. The certification was financially out of reach for a solo bootstrapped founder.
Prevention Actions
Stay ahead of UGC guidelines - subscribe to Apple Developer news, review policy updates quarterly
Build basic moderation tooling early (reported content queue, automated flagging) so scaling is not a cliff
Pursue Android (Google Play) in parallel - platform diversification reduces single-point-of-failure risk
Keep safety policies public and documented - makes compliance certification faster if required
🤷
LOW LIKELIHOOD

Scenario 6 - Nobody Actually Wanted It

The most honest failure
After 200 user interviews, 18 months of development, and 5 App Store rejections, the painful truth emerged: people say they want community connection, but their actual behavior tells a different story. The social friction of asking for help is a feature, not a bug - it filters out bad requests and maintains social capital. Demand was never really there. The people who "loved the idea" never actually posted. Usage peaked at 50 real sessions, then flatlined. Pivoting was too late.
Prevention Actions
Run Sean Ellis "very disappointed" test at exactly 100 users - if under 40%, pivot immediately
Watch behavior, not words: "I love this" in interviews means nothing if they never post
Define explicit pivot triggers now: if D7 retention under 10% at 200 users, the core loop is broken
Have a pivot hypothesis ready: same tech stack could serve hyperlocal commerce, skill exchange, elderly care

The Optimistic Flip

Every failure has a mirror success

The same forces that could kill Helpy - if navigated correctly - become its strongest competitive advantages. The flip side of each failure scenario is a reason why Helpy can win.
📭
Empty Feed - Cold Start
If we solve the cold start problem by activating one dense neighborhood, that neighborhood becomes a word-of-mouth engine. People tell their friends in the next building. Success spreads block by block - not through ads, but through lived experience.
🔐
Trust Incident
If we build a reputation for being the safest way to connect with neighbors - verified profiles, fast moderation, accountability - trust becomes our moat. Every other platform is reactive. We can be proactively safe by design.
💬
WhatsApp Copies It
WhatsApp cannot build a trust reputation system, verified identity, or urgency routing without alienating their privacy-first brand. The deeper we go on accountability and trust, the harder we are to copy with a lightweight feature.
🔋
Founder Burnout
If Helpy finds a co-founder or early team member, the combined energy is non-linear. Two people who genuinely believe in the mission can sustain far longer than one - and the product quality compounds with multiple perspectives.
🍎
App Store Policy Risk
Proactive safety compliance - built in from Day 1 - means Helpy can handle any new requirements without scrambling. Being a genuinely safe platform becomes a source of trust with Apple, users, and potential municipality partners.
🤷
No Real Demand
If the Sean Ellis test at 100 users shows 40%+ "very disappointed," we have confirmed genuine PMF in an underserved market. That data is the clearest signal to accelerate - to raise funding, expand cities, and build the growth engine while the window is open.
