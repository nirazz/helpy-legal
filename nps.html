Follow-Up Questions

For "Very disappointed" respondents
  • What would you use instead of Helpy if it were gone?
  • What do you love most about Helpy? What's the single best thing?
  • Who else in your neighborhood do you think would benefit from this?
For "Not disappointed" respondents
  • What's missing that would make you really love Helpy?
  • What would make this a weekly habit for you?
  • What was your last experience trying to use it - and where did it fall short?

When to Run Each Survey

50 active users
First Sean Ellis PMF Test
Early signal. Don't wait for more - you need to know if you're building something people want before investing further.
100 active users
First full NPS survey
Enough sample to get a statistically meaningful NPS. Run via push notification or in-app modal after request completion.
200 active users
Second Sean Ellis PMF Test
Compare to first run. Did the score move? What changed? Segment by helpers vs requesters.
Every quarter
Ongoing NPS pulse
Track NPS trend over time. A falling NPS signals a product regression before it shows up in churn numbers.
After each major feature
Mini targeted survey (3 questions max)
Ask users who experienced the new feature: Did it solve the problem? Was it intuitive? Would you miss it?

Reading Results Right

⚠️
Sample Bias
Happy users respond more. Churned users don't complete surveys. Your NPS will likely be inflated vs reality. Actively recruit both active and recently-churned users.
⏱️
Recency Bias
Timing matters. A user asked right after a successful match will score higher than one asked after a failed search. Send surveys 24 hours after key events, not immediately.
👥
Who Matters More Than What
Segment NPS by persona. Helpers and requesters have different needs. A 45 NPS from helpers with a 20 NPS from requesters means you need to fix the requester experience first.
