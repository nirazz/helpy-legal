👥 JTBD Framework

Jobs to Be Done

What job does a user hire Helpy to do? Understanding motivation beats understanding features.

Section 1

The JTBD Framework

People don't buy products - they hire them to do a job. The job has three dimensions: what they need to get done, how they need to feel, and what they need others to see. Miss any one of these and you lose the hire.

🔧
Functional Need
The practical outcome. "I need someone to watch my dog for an hour." Observable, measurable, the thing they'd put on a to-do list.
❤️
Emotional Need
How they need to feel during or after. "I need to feel less alone in this city." Harder to measure, impossible to ignore. This is the real why.
👥
Social Need
What they need others to perceive. "I want to be seen as the kind of person who helps." Identity and reputation are powerful motivators at both ends of the match.
Key insight for Helpy: Requesters and helpers have completely different jobs to be done. A product that tries to serve both with the same UX without understanding these differences will feel off to both. Every design decision should be traced back to whose job you're designing for.
Section 2

Primary Jobs - Requester

The person posting the request. They have a concrete problem AND a deeper underlying need. The surface request is rarely the whole story. Click any card to expand.

🙌
Requester - Job 1
"Help me get this task done without the awkwardness of asking"
▼
Functional
Get a specific small task completed - carrying furniture, reaching something, fixing a basic issue, getting a ride.
Emotional
Feel like it's OK to need help. Remove the shame and social calculation of asking a specific person directly.
Social
Not feel like a burden. The anonymized request format removes the direct "I owe you" dynamic of asking face to face.
Current Workaround
Not asking at all. Doing it the hard way alone. Or asking a close friend who lives far away.
Helpy's Solution
Post anonymously. Match is initiated by the helper, which removes the direct ask and the resulting obligation feeling.
🏠
Requester - Job 2
"Help me feel connected to my neighborhood"
▼
Functional
Find at least one neighbor whose name and face you know. Build a minimal social fabric in a dense, anonymous city.
Emotional
Feel less isolated. Know that if something went wrong, there's someone nearby who might actually respond.
Social
Be seen as a real member of the neighborhood community - not just a door number.
Current Workaround
WhatsApp building groups (passive, broadcast). Random encounters in the elevator. Nothing intentional.
Helpy's Solution
Each interaction creates a named, faceable connection. Profile cards show real people, not usernames. Ratings build a track record.
🔒
Requester - Job 3
"Reassure me it's safe to let someone help me"
▼
Functional
See enough information about a potential helper before accepting - photo, name, ratings, verification status.
Emotional
Feel secure enough to open the door, share a location, or accept help from someone you haven't met before.
Social
Trust the platform itself - know there's accountability. Ratings, verification, and report/block mechanisms signal "this is a real community."
Current Workaround
Only accepting help from people already known. Avoiding the situation entirely.
Helpy's Solution
Phone verification badge, Google/Apple auth (real identity), star ratings, block/report, community guidelines. Trust layers compound.
Section 3

Primary Jobs - Helper

The person swiping right. They're not just being altruistic - they have real needs too. Underestimating helper motivation is how platforms lose their supply side.

✨
Helper - Job 1
"Help me feel useful and contribute to something"
▼
Functional
Find a concrete task they can complete within their existing skills and availability. Not something that takes days - something doable today.
Emotional
Feel the satisfaction of having made a real difference to someone specific - not a donation to an abstract cause, but a named neighbor with a real need.
Social
Have proof of contribution. Ratings and streak counter let them see (and optionally share) that they're someone who shows up for their community.
Current Workaround
Volunteering at distant organizations. Waiting to be asked directly. No easy way to find small local needs.
Helpy's Solution
Browse feed of immediate nearby requests. Instant match on swipe. Star rating confirms the impact was real. Streak counter tracks consistency.
🖐
Helper - Job 2
"Help me meet my neighbors without the awkwardness"
▼
Functional
Have a structured reason to knock on a neighbor's door or approach someone on the street - a task, not small talk.
Emotional
Feel the warmth of a real human connection without the social risk of an unprompted approach.
Social
Be seen as a person who takes initiative in their community - not someone who walks past without engaging.
Current Workaround
Smiling in the elevator. Reading building WhatsApp groups without participating. Never meeting neighbors despite living 5 meters away for 3 years.
Helpy's Solution
The task is the social lubricant. After the help, a natural connection exists. Profile pictures and names make future encounters warmer.
🏆
Helper - Job 3
"Give me recognition for my contributions"
▼
Functional
See their history of help, their rating, their streak. Have something to point to when they think "I'm someone who helps."
Emotional
Feel seen and appreciated. Know that the help meant something - to the person helped and to the community they live in.
Social
Build a reputation as a reliable, trustworthy neighbor. The rating system is both intrinsic reward and social signal.
Current Workaround
No mechanism. Good deeds disappear into the void. No one knows who the helpful people in the building are.
Helpy's Solution
Star ratings visible on profile. Streak counter on ProfileScreen. "Fast responder" badge for high response rates. Community impact stats: Helped N neighbors.
Section 4

Underserved Jobs

Jobs Helpy currently does NOT fully serve. Each is a real opportunity - but scope discipline matters. These are v1.1+ unless user research shows them to be P0.

📅
Scheduled and Recurring Help
Some jobs aren't one-time - a weekly grocery run, regular dog walking, ongoing tutoring. Helpy only supports one-off requests today. Recurring needs have no solution.
v1.1 Candidate High frequency
📋
Neighborhood Skills Directory
Know who can help before you need them. "There's a doctor in building B, a lawyer on the 4th floor, someone who speaks English in unit 12." Proactive discovery, not reactive requesting.
v1.1 Candidate High retention
🎉
Group Activities with Neighbors
Not help requests - social initiatives. "Anyone want to walk at 7am?" "I'm ordering from X, want to split delivery?" Low-stakes group coordination that builds the neighborhood layer.
v2 Idea Scope risk
Section 5

The One Job That Matters Most

Strip away all the features, the analytics, the growth metrics. What does Helpy exist to do? Here it is in one story.

North Star - The Job Behind All Jobs
When everything breaks down at the worst moment possible...
When Dana posts a help request at 10pm because she has a job interview tomorrow and her baby won't stop crying and she just needs someone to watch the dog for an hour -

Helpy's job is: make her feel like she has a neighbor she can count on.
Not a transaction. Not a service. Not an app. A neighbor.
Every product decision should be tested against this: does it make the person on the other side feel less alone?
