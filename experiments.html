EXP-000A
Swipe vs Buttons
Adding explicit pill buttons alongside swipe gestures increases offer rate
Shipped
Variants
A Swipe gestures only
B Swipe + "Offer Help" / "Skip" pill buttons
Measured Outcome
Variant B increased offer rate by 40% - buttons reduced friction for users who didn't discover swipe gestures.
Decision
✅ Ship both

Keep swipe AND buttons. Buttons serve discovery users; swipe serves power users. Removing either reduces the total offer surface area.

EXP-000B
Toast vs Bottom Sheet
Bottom sheet confirmation drives more repeat offers than a toast pill
Shipped
Variants
A Dark pill toast ("Offer Sent")
B Bottom sheet with Undo, View in Chat, and request context
Measured Outcome
Bottom sheet increased undo usage 3x AND raised repeat offer rate. Users who saw context (request title + requester name) were more engaged.
Decision
✅ Ship bottom sheet

Undo + context creates a reward loop. Toast is invisible - bottom sheet makes the moment feel meaningful and gives a path forward (Chat). This is now the default.

How to Read Results

📐
Statistical Significance
Aim for p < 0.05 before deciding. With small samples (<200/variant), a result can look significant but be noise. Use a chi-square test for conversion rates.
🎯
Minimum Detectable Effect
Define upfront what improvement matters. A 2% lift on a 5% conversion rate isn't worth shipping. Aim for >10% relative improvement before calling it a win.
✨
Novelty Effect
New UI often gets a short-term boost just because it's different. Wait at least 2 weeks after launch before measuring - let the novelty wear off and see steady-state behavior.
🚫
Don't Peek Early
Checking results daily and stopping when you see what you want inflates false positives. Commit to your sample size upfront and don't declare a winner until you hit it.
